SCUDERIA FERRARI

TEAM PRINCIPAL: FREDERIC VASSEUR

DRIVERS: CHARLES LECLERC - CARLOS SAINZ JR.

ENGINE: FERRARI

CHAMPIONSHIPS: 16 (Constructors') - 15 (Drivers')

HISTORY:

Scuderia Ferrari is the most successful Formula One team in history, having won 16 Constructors' Championships and 15 Drivers' Championships. The team was founded by Enzo Ferrari in 1929 and has competed in every Formula One World Championship season since 1950. Ferrari's early years were marked by success, with the team winning its first Drivers' Championship in 1952 with Alberto Ascari and its first Constructors' Championship in 1953. The team continued to be competitive in the 1960s, winning Drivers' Championships with Phil Hill, John Surtees, and Mike Hawthorn. In the 1970s, Ferrari went through a period of decline, but the team returned to form in the 1980s with Drivers' Championships for Niki Lauda and Alain Prost. Ferrari's most successful period came in the late 1990s and early 2000s, when the team won five consecutive Constructors' Championships and four consecutive Drivers' Championships with Michael Schumacher. Since Schumacher's retirement in 2006, Ferrari has not won a Drivers' Championship, but the team has remained competitive, winning Constructors' Championships in 2007 and 2008. In 2022, the team won 3 races and finished third in the Constructors' Championship. Ferrari is one of the most popular and successful teams in Formula One. The team is known for its red cars, its passionate fans, and its legendary drivers. Ferrari is a true giant of Formula One and its history is one of the most fascinating in the sport.
