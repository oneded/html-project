MERCEDES AMG-PETRONAS F1

TEAM PRINCIPAL: TOTO WOLFF

DRIVERS: LEWIS HAMILTON - GEORGE RUSSELL

ENGINE: MERCEDES

CHAMPIONSHIPS: 8 (Constructors') - 9 (Drivers') (Included BrawnGP Championship)

HISTORY:

The Mercedes F1 team was founded in 2010, when Mercedes-Benz bought the Brawn GP team. Brawn GP had won the Constructors' Championship in 2009, but the team was struggling financially. Mercedes-Benz bought the team with the intention of returning Mercedes to the front of the grid.

The Mercedes team quickly became competitive, winning their first race in 2012. In 2014, Mercedes-Benz introduced a new engine for the F1 season. The engine was more powerful and efficient than the previous engines, and it helped Mercedes dominate the sport. Between 2014 and 2021, Mercedes won eight consecutive Constructors' Championships and seven consecutive Drivers' Championships. Lewis Hamilton won five Drivers' Championships during this period, and Nico Rosberg won one. In 2022, Mercedes struggled with their car and finished third in the Constructors' Championship. However, the team is still one of the most competitive in F1, and they are expected to be back at the front of the grid in 2023.
