ALFA ROMEO F1

TEAM PRINCIPAL: ALESSANDRO ALUNNI BRAVI

DRIVERS: VALTTERI BOTTAS - GUANYU ZHOU

ENGINE: FERRARI

CHAMPIONSHIPS: 0 (Constructors') - 2 (Drivers')

HISTORY:

Alfa Romeo has a long and storied history in Formula One, dating back to the very first World Championship season in 1950. The Italian manufacturer won the first two Drivers' Championships, with Nino Farina in 1950 and Juan Manuel Fangio in 1951. Alfa Romeo withdrew from Formula One after the 1951 season, but returned in 1979 as a customer engine supplier to Brabham. The team had a brief resurgence in the early 1980s, with Bruno Giacomelli scoring a pole position and a podium finish in 1980. However, Alfa Romeo withdrew from Formula One again after the 1987 season. In 2015, Alfa Romeo returned to Formula One as a commercial partner of Sauber Motorsport. The team was rebranded as Alfa Romeo Sauber F1 Team, and began using Ferrari engines. In 2019, Alfa Romeo became a full-fledged constructor in Formula One, and the team was renamed Alfa Romeo Racing. The team has had a mixed record since its return to Formula One, with its best finish being a fourth place in the Constructors' Championship in 2018. In 2022, Alfa Romeo Racing will be powered by a new engine from Ferrari. The team will also have a new driver lineup, with Valtteri Bottas joining from Mercedes and Zhou Guanyu becoming the first Chinese driver to race in Formula One. Alfa Romeo Racing will be hoping to improve on its recent results and challenge for regular points finishes in 2022.
