ALPINE F1

TEAM PRINCIPAL: ALESSANDRO ALUNNI BRAVI

DRIVERS: PIERRE GASLY - ESTEBAN OCON

ENGINE: RENAULT

CHAMPIONSHIPS: 2 (Constructors') - 1 (Drivers') [I DID NOT INCLUDE RENAULT F1 TEAM'S VICTORIES]

HISTORY:

The Alpine F1 Team is a French Formula One team that was founded in 1977 as Renault. The team has a long and storied history, winning two Constructors' Championships (1979, 1981) and one Drivers' Championship (1980, Alain Prost). Renault withdrew from Formula One in 1995 but returned as an engine supplier in 2001. In 2007, Renault bought the Benetton Formula One team and rebranded it as Renault F1. The team competed under that name until 2020, when it was rebranded as Alpine F1 Team. The Alpine F1 Team has had a mixed start to its life, with some success in 2022, finishing fifth in the Constructors' Championship. However, the team has the potential to be a competitive force in the years to come, with a strong driver line-up and continued investment from Renault.
